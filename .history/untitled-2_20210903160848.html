The Impact of Technology in Japan:

The Japanese government's implementation of stricter waste management regulations has forced residents to get creative in how they dispose of trash. The Japanese government's adoption of stricter waste management laws has prompted citizens to think outside the box when it comes to garbage disposal. In Japan you can find: Air Pollution, Water Pollution, Soil Contamination, Noise, Vibration, Offensive Odor, Ground Subsidence.

The reason Japan has problems with the environment happened when a mineral contamination case at the Ashio Copper Mine was recognized as Japan's first pollution case around the 20th year of the Meiji Period. After World War II, Japan significantly upgraded its industrial infrastructure and achieved heavy industrialization, particularly during the Showa period's 25th year. In result of the massive expansion in industrial manufacturing, a significant amount of gas was released. In addition, industries were built on the waterfront to increase production efficiency while concentrating pollution sources. Pollution-related illnesses were caused, including four major diseases like Minamata disease and Yokkaichi asthma.
